What happens automatically

After you click "Approve" in the admin dashboard, everything else happens on its own. The system emails them a $2,500 entry fee link. When they pay, it automatically sends them a second email with their annual dues link. When they pay that, it automatically creates their login account and emails them a temporary password. You don't have to touch it again.

Waiving the fee

When you approve an application, there's a "Waive Fee" option. Use this for VIPs, investors, or strategic partnerships. They skip the $2,500 entry fee AND the annual dues — their account is created immediately as a complimentary member.

The 80/20 split explained

When a member pays $8,000 for a round with Champ Bailey, CartPath keeps $1,600 (20%). You pay Champ $6,400 (80%) after the round is completed. Payment method is up to you — Venmo, wire transfer, or check. Record the payout in the admin dashboard so we have a paper trail.

Who can book whom?

Executive members can only book 3-Star and 4-Star athletes.
Chairman members can book everyone — including 5-Star Legends and Coaches.

What if a member doesn't pay the quote?

The Stripe link expires after 72 hours. If they don't pay, the booking dies. No charge, no follow-up required. If they want to rebook, they submit a new request.

Executive Assistants

Many of our members are CEOs and executives who have assistants managing their calendars. Instead of making the member do everything themselves, we let them invite their assistant to use CartPath on their behalf. The assistant gets their own separate login — they never have the member's password.

Assistants CAN
  • Submit round requests for the member
  • View the member's booking history
  • Browse the full athlete roster
  • Use the clubhouse chat
  • View the social feed and scores
  • See all the same athletes the member can see
Assistants CAN NEVER
  • Change the member's credit card or payment method
  • Edit the member's name, photo, or personal profile
  • Cancel the member's membership
  • View billing details (unless member specifically allows it)
  • Book over the dollar threshold without the member tapping "Approve"

How It Works — Step by Step

1
Member opens "Assistants" in the app
From the Account tab, the member taps "Assistants" and then "Invite an Assistant."
2
Member fills in the assistant's info
Name, email, and phone. They also choose what the assistant is allowed to do (toggle switches for booking, history, billing) and set a dollar threshold — for example, "$5,000 means any booking over $5K needs my approval first."
3
Assistant gets an email with a sign-up link
The email comes from hello@cartpathclub.com and says "[Member Name] has invited you to manage their CartPath account." The link expires after 7 days. The assistant clicks it and creates their own password.
4
Assistant logs in — sees a banner that says "Acting on behalf of [Member]"
The app looks the same as the member's view, but there's a gold banner at the top of key screens reminding them (and us) that they're acting on someone else's behalf. Everything they do is logged.
5
Assistant submits a round request
If the round estimate is under the threshold ($5K in this example), it goes straight to your review queue — same as if the member submitted it themselves. If it's over the threshold, it goes into a "Pending Member Approval" state first.
6
If over threshold: member gets a push notification
The member's phone buzzes: "Your assistant submitted a request for a round with Charles Barkley (~$10,000). Approve or decline?" The member taps one button. If they approve, it flows to your queue. If they decline, it dies.
7
Member can revoke access anytime
From the Assistants screen, the member taps "Revoke Access." The assistant's login stops working immediately. Everything the assistant did is still logged for the member to review.
Why this matters for CartPath

Most golf club apps make the CEO do everything themselves. These are busy people — they have someone who manages their calendar, books their restaurants, and coordinates their travel. If we make the CEO open the app and fill out a form every time they want to play golf, they won't use it. But if their assistant can do it for them, the booking happens the same day the CEO says "I want to play with Champ Bailey next Friday."

What YOU (Mayo) see when an assistant submits a booking

It looks exactly the same in your admin queue as a regular booking. There's a small "Assistant" tag on the booking card so you know it was submitted by an EA instead of the member directly, but the flow is identical — you quote it, the member (not the assistant) gets the payment link, the member pays, and you coordinate the round.

Complimentary athletes

Some legends may refuse to pay (and that's OK). You can flag any athlete as "complimentary" in the admin dashboard — they stay on the roster with no listing fee. Use this sparingly and only for athletes whose presence drives member signups.

Members never see this

The athlete pricing pages are hidden from the main website. They're only reachable via direct links that you send to athletes personally. There is no "For Athletes" section in the member portal, no pricing visible in the app. Members see athletes as "roster partners," not as paying subscribers.
